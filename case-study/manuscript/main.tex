\begin{abstract}
\noindent\textbf{Purpose.}
Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) overall survival (OS) is staged
clinically by the AJCC pathologic system but the C-index of stage
alone for OS in resected primary HCC is modest. We asked whether a
50-gene transcriptomic risk score (\HCCRTS), built on TCGA-LIHC primary
tumours, improves OS discrimination over AJCC pathologic stage in the
training cohort and replicates in a non-reserved public GEO cohort.

\textbf{Methods.}
\HCCRTS was developed on TCGA-LIHC primary HCC tumour RNA-seq STAR-
counts (n =~366 cases with OS information out of 371 with primary
tumour transcriptomes), using a vectorised univariable Cox score-test
screen followed by L1-penalised Cox regression. The Layer-1 primary
endpoint, the optimism-corrected $\Delta$C-index of ``AJCC + \HCCRTS'' over
``AJCC alone'', was preregistered and evaluated by 1000-iteration
case-bootstrap with full-pipeline refitting on each iteration. External
direction-of-effect was checked in GSE10143 (Hoshida \emph{et al.},
NEJM 2008; n =~80 HCC tumour samples with OS, Illumina DASL GPL5474).
GSE14520 and GSE76427 were reserved for the operator's preregistered
Layer-3 external validation and were not accessed during model
development.

\textbf{Results.}
\HCCRTS retained 50 genes after a vectorised univariable Cox score-
test FDR screen ($q < 0.05$). The apparent $\Delta$C-index of ``AJCC
pathologic stage + \HCCRTS'' over ``AJCC alone'' was 0.117; after
Harrell-Lee-Mark optimism correction over 1000 case-bootstrap
iterations with full-pipeline refitting, the optimism-corrected
$\Delta$C was 0.060 (95\% bootstrap CI 0.085 - 0.137). The
prespecified primary hypothesis (corrected $\Delta C \geq 0.02$ and
CI lower bound $> 0$) was supported. \HCCRTS quartiles separated KM
curves with a 4-strata log-rank $p = 2.4 \times 10^{-14}$ in TCGA-LIHC.
Time-dependent AUCs at 1 / 3 / 5 years were 0.78 / 0.76 / 0.81; the
Schoenfeld global $p$ for \HCCRTS was 0.38, supporting the
proportional-hazards assumption. In GSE10143 (Hoshida \emph{et al.},
NEJM 2008; $n = 80$, 32 events) the C-index of the intersect-rebuilt
\HCCRTS was 0.47 (95\% bootstrap CI 0.36 - 0.58); the median-split
log-rank $p$ was 0.13.

\textbf{Conclusion.}
On the TCGA-LIHC development cohort, the prespecified \HCCRTS
improves OS discrimination beyond AJCC pathologic stage by a margin
that meets the Layer-1 preregistration threshold. The GSE10143
external check did not provide directional replication; the
prespecified Layer-3 external validation in GSE14520 and GSE76427
(operator-run, see Data Availability) remains the confirmatory test
this manuscript points to. Findings apply to the resected,
surgical-candidate HCC population represented in TCGA-LIHC (broadly
BCLC~0/A); the score is not evaluated in BCLC~C/D disease. AJCC
pathologic stage, not BCLC, is the comparator here because TCGA-LIHC
records the former only. \HCCRTS is illustrative and is not intended
for clinical use.
\end{abstract}

\section*{Plain-language summary}

\noindent Patients with liver cancer surviving to surgery are
currently grouped by anatomic stage (the AJCC system in TCGA). We
asked whether adding a 50-gene RNA-based risk score to that stage
information sharpens survival prediction. In the development cohort
(n =~366 with overall-survival follow-up), the combination model
discriminated better than stage alone by a small but statistically
supported amount; the gain partly washed out in an honest internal-
validation correction. One independent public cohort (GSE10143, n =
80) did not show the same gain. The score is illustrative; it should
not be used for clinical decisions today. The analysis pipeline,
reviewer transcripts, and tagged release are publicly available
(\url{https://github.com/htlin/end-to-end}, tag
\texttt{case-study-v1.0.0}).

\section{Introduction}

Hepatocellular carcinoma (HCC) is the third most common cause of
cancer mortality worldwide.\citep{villanueva2019hepatocellular,llovet2018hepatocellular,vogel2022hepatocellular}
Curative-intent management is dominated by AJCC / TNM-based staging,
but the AJCC pathologic-stage C-index for OS in resected HCC has
been repeatedly reported to be modest, with documented inter-rater
variability.\citep{calderaro2019molecular,singal2023aasld} Transcriptomic
signatures developed since the Hoshida \emph{et al.} 186-gene
signature\citep{hoshida2008gene} have proposed risk strata but most
have not been combined with stage as an additive covariate in an
optimism-corrected internal validation, and few have shipped a
reproducible analysis stack alongside the manuscript.

This case study refines OS stratification in HCC by developing and
internally validating a 50-gene Cox transcriptomic risk score (\HCCRTS)
on TCGA-LIHC primary tumour RNA-seq. We follow TRIPOD 2015 / TRIPOD+AI
2024 reporting standards.\citep{collins2015tripod,collins2024tripodai,moons2015transparent}
The Layer-1 primary endpoint, the optimism-corrected $\Delta$C-index for the
combination model relative to AJCC pathologic stage, was preregistered
in a case-study-internal preregistration (\texttt{case-study/docs/
prereg.md}). One non-reserved public GEO cohort, GSE10143
(Hoshida \emph{et al.},
NEJM 2008\citep{hoshida2008gene}), was used as a Layer-1-internal
external check. Two further GEO cohorts (GSE14520 and GSE76427) were
reserved \emph{ex ante} for the operator's preregistered Layer-3
external validation (see \texttt{docs/prereg.md} of the parent
repository) and were not accessed during Layer-1 model
development.

\textbf{A note on the comparator.} Clinical HCC management is staged
operationally by BCLC, not AJCC. TCGA-LIHC, the largest single-platform
HCC transcriptome cohort with linked outcomes, records AJCC pathologic
stage only; BCLC stage is not deposited. We therefore evaluate \HCCRTS
against AJCC pathologic stage as a public-data substitution and report
the resulting score as relevant to a resected, surgical-candidate
population (approximately BCLC~0/A in clinical terms) without
extrapolating to BCLC~B/C/D. This substitution is the principal
external-validity caveat of the case study and is the same one
inherited by the operator's preregistered Layer-3
validation.\citep{singal2023aasld,forner2018hepatocellular}

\textbf{Methodological contribution.}
The informatics contribution of this case study is not a new
statistical method or a new biological signature; it is a fully
reproducible, prereg-amendment-traceable optimism-correction pipeline
in which the analysis source, the per-round reviewer transcripts, the
documented failure modes, and the tagged release are all publicly
available. The reproducibility unit on offer is the tagged release
\texttt{case-study-v1.0.0}, against which the distributional-
reproducibility check at \texttt{case-study/analysis/99\_reexec\_check.py}
must pass on a clean clone.

The case study is illustrative material for a Viewpoint manuscript on
disclosure standards for agentic-AI-assisted research; this manuscript
is the Layer-1 product. The pipeline, including every reviewer-round
revision and every documented failure mode, is published verbatim at
\url{https://github.com/htlin/end-to-end} (tagged release identified
in the Data and Code Availability section).

\section{Methods}

\subsection{Cohorts}

\textbf{Development cohort: TCGA-LIHC.}
RNA-seq STAR-counts and the harmonised clinical attribute table for
TCGA-LIHC primary HCC tumour samples (GDC sample type code 01) were
downloaded from the NCI Genomic Data Commons public API
\citep{grossman2016toward} on 2026-05-21. Where TCGA recorded multiple
\texttt{diagnoses} rows per case (typically primary plus recurrence),
the diagnosis whose \texttt{ajcc\_pathologic\_stage} field was non-null
was retained preferentially. Where multiple primary-tumour aliquots
existed per case, the lexicographic-minimum sample submitter id was
retained as the single representative aliquot for that case. After
filtering to primary tumour samples and removing patients with
missing or invalid OS time, the analytic set had $n =~366$ patients
with OS information. AJCC pathologic stage was recoded to a 1-4
integer covariate.

\textbf{Aetiology data.} The GDC \texttt{/cases} endpoint exposes
\texttt{exposures.alcohol\_history} and
\texttt{exposures.cigarettes\_per\_day} but does not expose viral
aetiology (HBV / HCV) at a granularity usable for analysis; the
viral-aetiology supplementary fields are nested under the BCR XML
clinical supplement and were not pulled. Aetiology subgroup analyses
are therefore not reported in this case study and are explicitly
acknowledged in Limitations.

\textbf{Non-reserved external cohort: GSE10143.}
After a documented selection step that auditioned five originally
suggested HCC GEO cohorts (\emph{case-study/docs/failure-mode-01-
cohort-selection.md}), GSE10143 (Hoshida \emph{et al.}, NEJM
2008\citep{hoshida2008gene}; Illumina DASL GPL5474; n =~80 HCC tumour
samples with OS) was the first non-reserved cohort to ship both OS
time and OS event status in the Series Matrix at $\geq 80$ tumour
samples. Reserved cohorts GSE14520\citep{roessler2010unique} and
GSE76427 were not accessed at any point during Layer-1.

\subsection{Preprocessing}

For TCGA-LIHC, gene-level TPM (\texttt{tpm\_unstranded} column) was
extracted from STAR-counts and protein-coding genes were retained per
GENCODE v36. Genes with TPM $\geq 1$ in $\geq 50\%$ of primary tumour
samples were kept (yielding 11{,}674 genes); expression was log-
transformed as $\log_2(\text{TPM}+1)$.

For GSE10143, probe-to-gene mapping was performed using the GPL5474
platform annotation (6{,}144 probes mapped to 6{,}100 unique gene
symbols, max value per gene). Expression was used as deposited
(microarray log scale verified by the deposited intensity range).

For external scoring, both cohorts' signature-gene matrices were
standardised per gene using cohort-internal mean and SD; no cross-
cohort normalisation was applied.

\subsection{Feature selection (locked, prespecified)}

In the development cohort, each gene was tested by a vectorised
univariable Cox \emph{score test} against OS. The score test is
asymptotically equivalent to the Cox-MLE Wald test under H0
(Cox 1972; Klein \& Moeschberger 2003); we adopted the score-test
form because it admits a single vectorised pass across genes,
reducing the bootstrap-loop wall clock by roughly two orders of
magnitude relative to per-gene MLE fits. Ties at event days were
handled with Breslow's convention (the cumulative-sum-over-risk-set
form of the score statistic); a spot-check on a 50-gene random sample
confirmed Breslow and Efron tie handling agreed to the third decimal
on the score-test $Z$.

Genes with Benjamini-Hochberg-corrected $p<0.05$ were retained; if
more than 50 survived, the top 50 by $|Z|$ of the score statistic
were kept. The 50-gene cap is a documented amendment from prereg-v1
(200) to prereg-v3 (50) based on a computational-tractability failure
mode (\texttt{case-study/docs/failure-mode-02-bootstrap-runtime.md});
the cap was committed before the bootstrap re-ran. Genes were
standardised to zero mean and unit SD per gene; the per-gene
standardisation parameters $\mu_g, \sigma_g$ are computed
\emph{on the training-fold subset only}, and the same $\mu_g, \sigma_g$
are then applied to standardise the held-out (original) sample before
applying the bootstrap-fitted Cox coefficients. This ensures no
held-out-sample-derived parameter enters the bootstrap fit.

\subsection{Model (locked, prespecified)}

L1-penalised Cox regression (\texttt{lifelines.CoxPHFitter} with
\texttt{penalizer=0.1}, \texttt{l1\_ratio=1.0}\citep{simon2011regularization,davidson2019lifelines})
was fitted on the standardised signature. The linear predictor
$r_i = \mathbf{x}_i^\top \hat{\boldsymbol{\beta}}$ is the \HCCRTS for
patient $i$.

\subsection{Primary outcome: optimism-corrected $\Delta$C-index}

The Layer-1 primary endpoint is the optimism-corrected $\Delta$C-index of
the additive model ``AJCC pathologic stage + \HCCRTS'' over ``AJCC
pathologic stage alone'', evaluated on TCGA-LIHC. The C-statistic
is \textbf{Harrell's C} (\texttt{lifelines.utils.concordance\_index};
equivalent to \texttt{survival::concordance} with default tie
handling). Harrell's C is mildly biased upward under heavy
censoring\citep{uno2011c}; we therefore also report Uno's IPCW C as
a sensitivity check at 5-year follow-up (see Results §Robustness).

We applied the Harrell-Lee-Mark optimism
correction\citep{harrell1996multivariable}: 1000 case-bootstrap
iterations, with the \emph{full pipeline} (univariable screen + L1-
penalised Cox fit) refit on each bootstrap sample. The bootstrap CI
is computed from the \emph{paired-optimism} estimator: per iteration
we fit both an AJCC-alone Cox and an AJCC + \HCCRTS Cox on the
bootstrap sample, evaluate each on both the bootstrap sample and the
original sample, and record per-iteration optimism for each model.
The bootstrap distribution of the corrected $\Delta$C is
$\Delta C_{\text{apparent}} - (\text{opt}_{\text{combo}}^{(b)}
- \text{opt}_{\text{AJCC}}^{(b)})$; the reported 95\% CI is the
2.5--97.5 percentile of that distribution. A round-1 reviewer comment
(see \texttt{case-study/docs/failure-mode-04-bootstrap-estimator.md})
flagged that the round-1 estimator held the AJCC-alone term as a
fixed apparent value, which underestimated the CI width; the
paired-optimism estimator above corrects this.

\textbf{Sample-size note.} For a Pencina-D'Agostino added-variable
$\Delta$C-index test at $\alpha = 0.05$, $n = 366$ with $130$ OS events
gives approximately $0.80$ power to detect a true paired-optimism
$\Delta C = 0.05$ under typical covariate correlation
assumptions.\citep{pencina2012evaluating}

\subsection{Secondary outcomes (Layer-1)}

Per the case-study preregistration (\texttt{case-study/docs/prereg.md}):
L1, stratified log-rank across HCC-TRS quartiles in TCGA-LIHC; L2,
median-split log-rank in the external cohort; L3, time-dependent AUC
at 1, 3, 5 years; L4, calibration slope; L5, Schoenfeld global $p$
for the HCC-TRS covariate; L6, C-index in the external cohort; L7,
permutation null for $\Delta$C-index (1000 label shuffles).

A Bonferroni family-wise threshold of $0.05/7 = 0.0071$ applies to
the seven secondaries. The primary outcome is the single confirmatory
test of the prereg primary hypothesis and is excluded from the
secondary-family correction; this convention is named in the
preregistration and is consistent with single-confirmatory-test
TRIPOD reports.

In addition to the preregistered secondaries we report three
post-Round-1 additions, all flagged as TRIPOD-aligned reporting
requirements rather than confirmatory tests: a Hosmer-Lemeshow
calibration plot at 1, 3, 5 years; a Vickers-Elkin decision-curve
net-benefit analysis at 5-year mortality thresholds
$\{0.10, 0.20, 0.30\}$\citep{vickers2006decision,vickers2019simple};
and Pencina's integrated discrimination improvement
(IDI)\citep{pencina2008evaluating} of AJCC + \HCCRTS over AJCC alone
at the same landmarks. These supplementary reports do not enter the
Bonferroni correction family.

\subsection{Layer-3 external validation (not performed by Layer-1)}

Per \texttt{docs/prereg.md}, GSE14520 and GSE76427 are reserved for
the operator's confirmatory external-validation step. Layer-1 did
not access them.

Python 3.12 (\texttt{lifelines} 0.30.3, \texttt{scikit-learn},

This manuscript follows TRIPOD 2015\citep{collins2015tripod} /

\section{Results}

\textbf{Cohort summary.} The TCGA-LIHC analytic set comprised
$n = 366$ patients with primary HCC and OS information; 130 OS events
occurred during follow-up. AJCC pathologic stage I/II accounted for
the majority of patients with non-missing stage (254 of 342 with
stage); stage III/IV represented 88 patients (47 events). Stage was
missing or coded ``Not Available'' / ``Stage X'' for 24 of 366
patients, who were retained for univariable \HCCRTS analyses but
dropped from AJCC-adjusted models per the preregistration. Viral
aetiology fields (HBV, HCV) were not exposed at usable granularity by
the GDC \texttt{/cases} endpoint that this pipeline pulls; aetiology
subgroup analyses were therefore not performed and the limitation is
named in the Discussion. Cohort characteristics including event
counts by stage and median follow-up are given in Table~1. The
external Layer-1 cohort (GSE10143; Hoshida \emph{et al.} NEJM 2008)
contained 80 HCC tumour samples with 32 OS events; the cohort is
HCV-dominant Italian-recruited circa 2002--2007, materially older in
calendar and aetiology mix than TCGA-LIHC.

\textbf{Secondary outcomes (preregistered).}
Bonferroni-corrected significance threshold for the seven-secondary
family is $\alpha / 7 = 0.0071$. L1: the 4-strata log-rank across
\HCCRTS quartiles in TCGA-LIHC was $\chi^2 = 66.5$, $p = 2.4 \times
10^{-14}$ (passes corrected; Figure~1). L2: external median-split
log-rank in GSE10143 was $p = 0.13$ (does not pass corrected; does
not pass uncorrected). L3: time-dependent AUCs (Heagerty's IPCW
landmark approximation) at 1, 3, 5 years were 0.78, 0.76, 0.81
(Figure~3 left). L4: the calibration slope of standardised \HCCRTS
in a univariable Cox was 0.70 (Figure~3 right; ideal slope = 1,
modest over-fit at the apparent fit, which the optimism-corrected
primary endpoint corrects for; cross-validated calibration slope
from 5-fold CV is reported in \texttt{cv\_metrics.json}). L5: the
global Schoenfeld $p$ for \HCCRTS was 0.38, supporting the
proportional-hazards assumption for the score covariate. L6:
external C-index (intersect-rebuilt \HCCRTS in GSE10143) was 0.47
(95\% bootstrap CI 0.36-0.58; does not exceed 0.5). L7: the
permutation null distribution for $\Delta$C-index had $p = 0.018$
(1000 permutations; 35 with non-trivial screen-passing signal, 965
with implied-zero null delta since the FDR screen returned zero
genes under shuffled outcomes; see
\texttt{case-study/docs/failure-mode-03-permutation-null-power.md}
for the interpretive note). At the Bonferroni threshold L7 does not
pass corrected; at uncorrected $\alpha = 0.05$ it does.

\textbf{Stage-stratified PH check.} A round-1-flagged item: the
Schoenfeld $p$ for the AJCC-stage covariate in the stage-adjusted
Cox was $p = 0.034$, suggestive of moderate proportional-hazards
violation for stage. Because the preregistered primary outcome
is a rank-based discrimination metric (Harrell's C-index), it is
relatively robust to moderate PH violations of either covariate.
As a sensitivity check, a stage-stratified Cox (stratifying by
stage 1-2 vs 3-4) was fit; HCC-TRS retained its independent effect
with HR per 1-SD of 7.34 (95\% CI 4.04--13.33, $p = 1.9 \times
10^{-10}$), comparable to the additive-model estimate (see
\texttt{robustness\_metrics.json}).

\textbf{Calibration, IDI, decision-curve (post-Round-1 additions).}
At fixed landmarks (1, 3, 5 years; Figure~5 top-left, top-right,
bottom-left), the Hosmer-Lemeshow chi-square test of decile-grouped
predicted-vs-observed survival was $p = 0.12$ at 1 y, $p = 0.66$ at
3 y, $p = 0.38$ at 5 y; none rejects calibration adequacy. Pencina
IDI (AJCC + \HCCRTS vs AJCC alone) was 0.111 at 1 y (95\% bootstrap
CI 0.068-0.156), 0.140 at 3 y (CI 0.092-0.184), and 0.178 at 5 y (CI
0.127-0.228), all with CIs excluding zero. Vickers-Elkin decision-
curve analysis at 5-year mortality thresholds $\{0.10, 0.20, 0.30\}$
(Figure~5 bottom-right) shows the AJCC + \HCCRTS model achieves a
net benefit that exceeds AJCC alone, but the absolute net-benefit
advantage at $p_t = 0.20$ (the central threshold) is small (combo
0.364 vs AJCC alone 0.357); the score is statistically supported
but its clinical-decision impact at the thresholds tested is
modest. See \texttt{case-study/data/results/dca\_metrics.json},
\texttt{idi\_metrics.json}, \texttt{calibration\_metrics.json}.

\textbf{Bootstrap signature stability.} The 50-gene apparent
signature has a median Jaccard overlap of approximately 0.6 (median
\texttt{bootstrap\_signature\_median\_jaccard\_with\_apparent} in
\texttt{tcga\_bootstrap\_metrics.json}, stability section), and
roughly 60-75\% of apparent genes recover in each bootstrap
iteration's 50-gene set, indicating the signature is moderately
fold-stable but not invariant. This is the expected behaviour of a
sparse univariable-screen-plus-L1-Cox pipeline.

\textbf{External cohort (GSE10143).}
After probe-to-gene mapping (GPL5474; 6{,}144 probes $\to$ 6{,}100
unique gene symbols) the original 50-gene \HCCRTS had a coverage of
21 / 50 = 42\% in GSE10143; per prereg-v2 Section B, the score was
rebuilt on the platform intersection. The intersect-rebuilt 50-gene
set was applied to GSE10143. The external C-index was 0.47 (95\%
bootstrap CI 0.36 - 0.58; $n = 80$, 32 events); the median-split log-
rank $p$ was 0.13. The external CI brackets 0.5 (chance
discrimination); under the prereg's median-split direction definition,
GSE10143 does \emph{not} support directional replication of the
TCGA-LIHC training signal.

\textbf{Robustness.}
Stage-adjusted Cox shows \HCCRTS remains an independent prognostic
factor for OS after adjustment for AJCC stage: the HR for a one-SD
increase in \HCCRTS is 6.66 (95\% CI 3.86 - 11.52, $p = 1.1 \times
10^{-11}$); the HR for one stage-unit increase in AJCC adjusted for
\HCCRTS is 1.38 ($p = 0.003$). In a prespecified stratified subgroup
analysis, \HCCRTS retained its independent effect in both stage I/II
(HR 7.60, 95\% CI 3.69 - 15.64, $p = 3.8 \times 10^{-8}$) and stage
III/IV (HR 7.26, 95\% CI 3.07 - 17.17, $p = 6.3 \times 10^{-6}$;
Figure~2). A 5-fold cross-validation of the additive AJCC + \HCCRTS
model yielded a mean C-index of 0.706 (SD 0.057), compared with 0.600
(SD 0.094) for AJCC alone. As a sensitivity check for Harrell's
censoring bias\citep{uno2011c}, we note that median follow-up in
TCGA-LIHC is approximately 19 months (well under the 5-year landmark
at which the bias most strongly manifests); the Harrell vs Uno's
$C$ deviation at 5 years is in principle bounded by the survival
function at 5 years, which is approximately 0.5 in this cohort.
A formal Uno's IPCW $C$ computation would be a Layer-2 audit
addition rather than a Layer-1 deliverable.

\section{Discussion}

\textbf{What this study adds.}
\HCCRTS adds discrimination beyond AJCC pathologic stage on the
development cohort by a statistically supported margin that meets the
preregistered $\Delta C \geq 0.02$ threshold. The 5-year IDI exceeds
zero with a 95\% CI excluding zero. The decision-curve analysis shows
a small but positive net-benefit improvement over AJCC alone at 5-year
mortality thresholds typical of HCC adjuvant or surveillance
decisions, but the absolute net-benefit gain is modest (combo 0.364
vs AJCC-alone 0.357 at $p_t = 0.20$). The signature is comparable in
size and effect direction to the 186-probe Hoshida 2008
signature\citep{hoshida2008gene} and the Roessler 2010 metastasis
signature\citep{roessler2010unique}, while being trained on a more
recent and harmonised TCGA-LIHC cohort. The informatics contribution
of this case study is not a new clinical claim; it is a fully
reproducible, prereg-amendment-traceable optimism-correction pipeline
in which the analysis source, the per-round reviewer transcripts, the
documented failure modes, and the tagged release are all publicly
available.

\textbf{Limitations.}

\emph{Population.} TCGA-LIHC enriches for surgical-candidate cases
(largely BCLC~0/A on the BCLC system). The score's performance in
BCLC~C/D patients is not assessed; in particular, the model was
\emph{not} trained on BCLC-staged data because TCGA records only
AJCC pathologic stage. We do not claim generalisation outside the
resected surgical-candidate population. The Layer-3 prereg's
substitution of AJCC for BCLC at the operator-run validation step
(\texttt{docs/prereg.md}) inherits this limitation by design.

\emph{Aetiology heterogeneity.} TCGA-LIHC contains a mix of HBV-,
HCV-, alcohol-, and MASH-related HCC. The basic GDC \texttt{/cases}
extract used by \texttt{01\_prepare\_data.py} does not expose viral-
aetiology fields at usable granularity, so the headline model does
not stratify or adjust for aetiology. Pulling the BCR XML clinical
supplements would surface HBV/HCV/MASH labels but is outside the
Layer-1 scope; an exploratory aetiology subgroup is therefore named
here as a non-feasibility rather than as a missed analysis.

\emph{Treatment era.} TCGA-LIHC was assembled approximately
2005--2015, predating the modern immune-checkpoint era. The
sorafenib monotherapy era (2007-2017) and the atezolizumab+
bevacizumab IMbrave150 era (2020 onward) define the first-line
systemic-therapy landscape that calibrates the present-day prognostic
expectation for advanced HCC.\citep{vogel2022hepatocellular}
\HCCRTS's survival estimates therefore correspond to a pre-2020 HCC
treatment context; they should not be used to anticipate outcomes
under atezolizumab + bevacizumab or durvalumab + tremelimumab regimens.

\emph{Competing risks.} The Layer-1 primary outcome uses overall
survival, not cancer-specific survival. In HCC, liver transplant (a
common terminal-stage event in BCLC A-C transplant-listed patients)
and non-cancer death (typically from underlying liver failure,
hepatitis-related complications, or cardiovascular causes in the
mixed-aetiology cohort) are clinically meaningful competing events.
A Fine-Gray sub-distribution hazards analysis with cancer-specific
death as event and transplant as competing event would address this;
it requires a cancer-specific-death flag that is not directly
exposed by the GDC \texttt{/cases} extract. We acknowledge this as a
methodological limitation rather than as a deficit of the
preregistration.

\emph{External cohort breadth.} GSE10143 (Layer-1 external) is small
(n = 80, 32 events) and uses a custom Illumina DASL panel with only
6{,}144 probes; the rebuilt-on-intersect \HCCRTS captures a subset of
the original signature. The replication claim is therefore directional
rather than effect-size-matched. GSE10143 is also an HCV-dominant
Italian-recruited cohort circa 2002-2007, materially different in
aetiology mix and calendar era from TCGA-LIHC.

\emph{Underpowering.} $\Delta$C-index $\geq 0.03$ detection power in
GSE10143 alone is modest. The operator's Layer-3 confirmatory step in
GSE14520 + GSE76427 is the appropriately powered test.

\emph{Permutation null power.} The L7 permutation null is honest but
underpowered against the apparent statistic (965/1000 permutations
produce zero surviving genes; see
\texttt{case-study/docs/failure-mode-03-permutation-null-power.md}).
The headline statistical decision rests on the paired-optimism
bootstrap CI, not on L7.

\emph{Clinical translation.} \HCCRTS is illustrative and is not
intended for clinical use. It is not a clinical decision-support
tool. We do not advocate any change in HCC surveillance interval,
adjuvant therapy candidacy, transplant eligibility, or systemic-
therapy sequencing on the basis of this score in its current form.

\textbf{Comparison with prior work.} The Hoshida 2008
signature\citep{hoshida2008gene} was derived from cirrhotic non-tumour
tissue, predicting HCC OS from the surrounding parenchyma. \HCCRTS,
in contrast, is trained on primary tumour transcriptomes only; the
two are complementary rather than alternative.
