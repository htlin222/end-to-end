## What the manuscript reports

This case study develops a 50-gene transcriptomic overall-survival risk
score (HCC-TRS) for hepatocellular carcinoma, trained on TCGA-LIHC
primary tumour RNA-seq (n approximately 366) and internally validated
by 1000-iteration optimism-corrected case-bootstrap with full-pipeline
refitting on each iteration. We report:

- the apparent and Harrell-Lee-Mark optimism-corrected
  Delta-C-index of "AJCC pathologic stage + HCC-TRS" over "AJCC
  pathologic stage alone",
- time-dependent AUC at 1, 3, 5 years and Cox calibration slope,
- a 1000-iteration permutation null for the Delta-C-index,
- direction-of-effect replication in a non-reserved public GEO cohort
  (GSE10143, Hoshida et al, NEJM 2008; n = 80, 32 OS events).

Two further GEO cohorts (GSE14520, GSE76427) are reserved for a
preregistered, operator-run Layer-3 external validation
(`docs/prereg.md` of the linked repository) and were not accessed
during model development.

## Why this fits JCO CCI

The manuscript is a TRIPOD-aligned clinical-prediction Original Report
that uses publicly available genomic data (TCGA-LIHC RNA-seq + one GEO
cohort) and ships a fully reproducible pipeline (analysis scripts,
reviewer-loop transcripts, manuscript LaTeX) at a public release tag
(`case-study-v1.0.0`) of a public GitHub repository.

The contribution is informatics-flavoured rather than mechanistic:
(1) a vectorised univariable-Cox score-test screen that makes
1000-iteration bootstrap with full-pipeline refitting computationally
tractable on a laptop; (2) an optimism-corrected Delta-C-index reported
with a percentile bootstrap CI and permutation null; (3) a single non-
reserved external cohort with a documented probe-overlap rebuild rule.

## Bounded clinical translation

The manuscript states explicitly that HCC-TRS is illustrative and is
**not** intended for clinical use. We do not advocate any change in
HCC surveillance interval, adjuvant therapy candidacy, or transplant
eligibility on the basis of this score. The score's deliberate
boundedness is part of its honesty contract; the Discussion section
documents the limitations (TCGA cohort era and surgical-candidate
enrichment, aetiology heterogeneity, absence of competing-risks
adjustment, small external cohort) without softening them.

## Generative-AI disclosure

This work is also the empirical artefact for a parallel Viewpoint
manuscript on disclosure standards for agentic-AI-assisted research.
The analysis pipeline, the manuscript text, the methods description,
and the four reviewer-subagent rounds were produced by an autonomous
Claude Code session (Anthropic Claude Opus 4.7, 1M-context variant);

release tag. A per-tool, per-task disclosure log is published at
`docs/ai-usage-disclosure.md` of the linked repository, with the
exact orchestration prompts in `prompts/`. No figure or image was
generated by AI.

The disclosure is more permissive than the Viewpoint argues current
journal policy explicitly permits; we make it openly because the
case study is bundled with a Viewpoint that argues for exactly this
level of detail. The case-study Methods section discloses the AI
usage in the form *JCO CCI*'s author instructions require.

## Reproducibility statement

The manuscript ships with a tagged GitHub release
(`case-study-v1.0.0`) containing every analysis script, every
reviewer-round transcript, every documented failure-mode file, the
manuscript LaTeX source, and the compiled artefacts. A clean clone,
`uv sync`, and sequential execution of
`case-study/analysis/01_prepare_data.py` through
`case-study/analysis/04_figures.py` and
`case-study/analysis/05_dca_idi_calibration.py` reproduce every
number in the manuscript. The reproducibility-check script
`case-study/analysis/99_reexec_check.py` asserts that the committed
`case-study/data/results/` JSON scalars and numeric tables match
the pipeline's re-execution within prespecified tolerances
(deterministic fields 1e-6; bootstrap-derived fields 5e-2); the
committed artefacts pass the check at the `case-study-v1.0.0`
release tag.

## Pre-registration

The Layer-1 analytic design is preregistered in
`case-study/docs/prereg.md`, committed before any model was fitted.
Two documented amendments (`prereg-v2.md`, `prereg-v3.md`) were each
committed before the affected code path ran, in response to specific
failure modes (extended GEO candidate pool;
bootstrap-runtime-budget-driven signature cap reduction). Neither
amendment touches the primary outcome, the primary hypothesis, or the
correction family.
